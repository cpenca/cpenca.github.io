Loved Poems

These are some poems that have left a lasting impression on me, have impacted my life, or have influenced my language and the words I use to experience life.

Links to external sites are impermanent. As such I've tried to use a photo of the poem from a book or a screen shot from a website.

John Berryman

John Berryman was the first poet I truly studied. He was considered one of the confessional poets. "I didn't want to be like Yeats; I wanted to be Yeats"

Dream Song 1h — The first poem in what became an expand series of poems. Introduces Henry, a stand-in for the poet, as well as the sense of loss the permeates the work.
Hope is the thing with feathers — An uplifting metaphor for resilience and optimism.
James Tate

James Tate turned me on to the idea that poetry can be funny,surreal,and also moving. I came across him early in highschool and probably owe much of my interest in poetry to him.

The Lost Pilot - James Tate's father was killed in a bombing mission during WWII. Tate was very young when he died. I think of the final lines often when the world serves me misfortune
Hope is the thing with feathers — An uplifting metaphor for resilience and optimism.
